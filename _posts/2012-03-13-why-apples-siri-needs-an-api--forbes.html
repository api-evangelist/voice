Last week, Apple‘s announcement of a new iPad landed with the thud of a missed opportunity.The device boasted a sharper screen, a faster processor and a better camera—all nice improvements noticeably lacking in “wow.” Even David Pogue, the influential New York Times personal tech columnist, was reduced to writing about new versions of Garage Band and iPhoto.If only the company had listened to Woz.Steve Wozniak, Apple’s co-founder wrote in an email to Forbes that he had hoped that Apple would take steps to allow Siri, its ground-breaking intelligent personal assistant, to work better with other apps.
