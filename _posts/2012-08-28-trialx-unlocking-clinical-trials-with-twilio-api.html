For patients coming to terms with a new diagnosis or a reoccurrence of their disease, clinical trials represent a rare form of hope.For doctors, clinical trials are a critical stop on the road to a cure.But for years, matching patients with clinical trials of new treatments currently under investigation has been an exercise in futility.Trial investigators usually have to rely on ﬁle cabinets full of paper records to ﬁnd suitable patients and few tools, if any, exist to make it easier for patients to ﬁnd clinical trials near them.Consequently, four out of ﬁve clinical trials are delayed and 50% of the delays are due to participant recruitment challenges.
